# Campaign Analytics — Key Insights

## Phase 2 — SQL Analysis

### Insight 1: High conversion ≠ high revenue
- C014 had the best conversion rate (19.42%) but lowest revenue (₹10,87,644)
- C001 had 15.44% conversion but generated ₹43,83,036 — 4x more
- Root cause: C014 reached lower income segments who spent less per transaction
- Business implication: Optimizing for conversion rate alone is misleading

### Insight 2: Telemarketing has highest cost, inconsistent return
- C016 spent ₹77,819 — highest budget in dataset
- C012 spent ₹68,427 — generated only ₹13,20,158 revenue
- C001 Email spent ₹9,012 — generated ₹43,83,036
- Business implication: Email campaigns show better cost efficiency

### Insight 3: Email campaigns deliver superior ROI despite lower budgets
- C003 Email (budget ₹5,520) → ROI 52,200%
- C016 Telemarketing (budget ₹77,819) → ROI 2,200%
- Telemarketing spends 14x more but delivers 24x lower ROI
- Root cause: High operational cost of telemarketing erodes returns
- Business implication: Reallocate telemarketing budget to Email and Push

### Insight 4: Premium segment — 5.6% of customers, 75% of revenue
- 112 Premium customers generate ₹2.86 Crore — 75.3% of total revenue
- Average Premium transaction: ₹1,77,978 vs Mass average ₹8,185
- Premium converts at 53% — highest engagement of all segments
- Mass has 10x more customers but generates 14x less revenue
- Business implication: Acquiring and retaining Premium customers 
  should be the single highest priority for campaign targeting

  ### Insight 5: Revenue trend shows growth with Q3/Q4 strength
- December highest transaction volume (476)
- October highest revenue month (₹1.12 Crore)
- February weakest month — potential target for campaign push
- Note: Synthetic data — real patterns would reflect Indian festival 
  calendar and financial year cycles

### Insight 6: The conversion rate trap — C014 case study
- C014 ranks #1 in conversion rate (39.46%)
- C014 ranks #20 (last) in revenue (₹10,87,644)
- C014 ranks #15 in ROI (6,000%)
- Optimizing campaigns for conversion rate alone would lead to 
  budget being allocated to the worst revenue-generating campaign
- Business implication: ROI must be the primary optimization metric,
  not conversion rate

### Insight 7: Top 3 ROI campaigns are all Email — under ₹12,000 budget each
- C003, C001, C007 — all Email campaigns
- Combined budget: ₹26,456
- Combined revenue: ₹1.08 Crore
- Average ROI: 43,500%
- Business implication: Scale Email campaigns aggressively. 
  Cap Telemarketing budget until ROI improves.
